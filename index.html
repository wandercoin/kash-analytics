What is this?

This is a free, independent dashboard that shows useful information about Kash — a prediction market where people guess whether future events will happen (who wins a match, where a crypto price lands, pop-culture questions, and so on). It reads only public data and runs entirely in your browser. Nothing to install, no sign-up, no wallet. It only reads data — it never places bets or changes anything. Not affiliated with Kash.

New to prediction markets?

In a prediction market you buy “Yes” or “No” on a question like “Will Team X win?”. The price runs from 0% to 100% and shows how likely the crowd thinks the answer is “Yes”. A market at 70% means the crowd thinks there’s about a 70% chance. When the event finishes, the market resolves to Yes or No. This dashboard just helps you understand those markets — it doesn’t ask you to bet.

The tabs, in plain words

InsightsThe big picture — which topics get the most money and attention, and which markets are the busiest.
Resolution CalibrationChecks whether Kash’s predictions actually come true. If markets priced at 70% really happen about 70% of the time, the pricing is accurate.
🎯 ConsistencyA quick health check: for a question with many answers, do all the chances add up to 100%? They should — and here they do.
MarketsA searchable list of every live market. Click any card to open that market on Kash.

How to use it

  1. Click the tabs at the top to switch between views.
  2. Some tabs (Calibration, Consistency) run a calculation when you open them or press their button. A spinner shows the progress — just give it a few seconds.
  3. Press ↻ Reload (top right) any time to refresh with the latest data.
  4. On Markets, type in the search box or use the filters to find a market, then click it to open on Kash.
  5. On a slow connection the numbers may take a few seconds to appear — that’s normal.
All numbers come live from Kash’s public API (app.kash.bot/api). If a page looks empty, press Reload or wait a moment for the data to load.

Is Kash's pricing calibrated? (predicted % vs actual outcome)

Click "Run calibration" — it samples resolved markets + price history (in your browser).
Tests the core trustless-resolution claim: do markets that traded at ~70% actually resolve YES ~70% of the time? Brier score: lower = better calibrated (0 = perfect, 0.25 = coin-flip). Needs enough resolved markets with price history.

Do multi-outcome markets add up to 100%?

Click "Scan" — fetches each multi-outcome market and sums its outcome probabilities (in your browser).
A market's mutually-exclusive outcomes should sum to 100%. >100% = overround — a built-in house edge (don't buy the field). <100% = underround — a theoretical buy-every-outcome arb, but only real if the gap beats 2% fee × number of outcomes + slippage. This is the one view here that surfaces a tradable signal, not just a transparency flag.
